## 1. What is AssetHub?

AssetHub is your organization's **asset tracking system**. It keeps a record of every piece of equipment, tool, or device your team owns — and who is using what, right now.

With AssetHub you can:

- **Find** any asset instantly by name, type, or location
- **Check out** equipment you need and return it when you're done
- **Scan** QR codes on physical assets with your phone camera
- **Request maintenance** when something is broken
- **Get notified** when assets assigned to you change status

## 2. Getting Started — Logging In

### First-time login

You will receive an invitation email from your organization's administrator.

1. Open the link in the invitation email
2. Set a password when prompted
3. You're in!

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the invitation email and the set-password screen.

### Regular login

1. Go to your organization's AssetHub address (e.g., `https://assethub.yourcompany.com`)
2. Enter your **email address** and **password**
3. Click **Sign in**

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the login page.

### Forgot your password?

1. Click **Forgot password?** on the login screen
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your inbox for a reset link
4. Click the link and set a new password

> **Note:** Reset links expire after 1 hour. If yours expired, simply request another one.

## 3. The Dashboard

After logging in, you land on the **Dashboard** — your home base.

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Full-width screenshot of the Dashboard page with KPI cards and status chart visible.

### What you'll see

| Section | What it shows |
|---------|--------------|
| **KPI Cards** (top row) | Total assets · In Service · In Repair · Warranty Expiring soon |
| **Status Breakdown** | A bar or chart showing all asset statuses at a glance |
| **Recent activity** | Latest check-outs, check-ins, and maintenance events |

### Clickable cards

Each KPI card is a **shortcut**. Clicking it takes you directly to the filtered asset list:

- Click **"In Service"** → see all active, available assets
- Click **"In Repair"** → see assets currently under maintenance
- Click **"Warranty Expiring (30d)"** → see assets whose warranty ends within 30 days

## 4. Finding & Viewing Assets

### Browse all assets

Click **Assets** in the left sidebar to see every asset in your organization.

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the Assets list page showing the search bar, status filter dropdown, and a table of assets with columns: Name, Type, Location, Status, Warranty.

### Search

Type in the **search box** at the top of the assets page. AssetHub searches across asset names, serial numbers, and tags as you type.

### Filter by status

Use the **Status** dropdown to narrow down to:
- All statuses
- In Service
- In Repair
- Retired
- Lost
- Checked Out

### Filter by location

Use the **Location** dropdown to see assets in a specific room, building, or site.

### Warranty filter chip

If you clicked the "Warranty Expiring" dashboard card, you'll see a red **"Warranty expiring (30d)"** chip at the top of the list. Click the **✕** on the chip to clear this filter.

### View an asset's details

Click any asset row to open its detail page. You'll see:

- **Basic info:** Name, serial number, asset type, category
- **Location:** Where the asset lives
- **Status:** Current state
- **Checkout history:** Who had it and when
- **Custom fields:** Any extra info your admin has set up (e.g., model number, purchase date)
- **Maintenance history:** Past repair records

## 5. Checking Out an Asset

Checking out means you're officially taking responsibility for an asset.

1. Find the asset (search or browse)
2. Open the asset detail page
3. Click **Check Out**
4. Confirm in the dialog — add a note if needed (e.g., "Taking to conference room B")
5. Click **Confirm**

The asset status changes to **Checked Out** and is linked to your name.

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the Check Out confirmation dialog.

> **Note:** You need the *checkout* permission to check out assets. If you don't see the Check Out button, contact your administrator.

## 6. Returning (Checking In) an Asset

When you're done with an asset, check it back in so others can use it.

1. Find the asset (it will show as "Checked Out" with your name)
2. Open the asset detail page
3. Click **Check In**
4. Add any notes (e.g., "Returned in good condition")
5. Click **Confirm**

The asset status returns to **In Service** and is available for others.

## 7. Scanning an Asset with a QR Code

Every asset in AssetHub can have a **QR code** printed on it. Scanning it is the fastest way to pull up an asset.

### How to scan

1. In the sidebar, tap **Scan**
2. Allow camera access when prompted
3. Point your camera at the QR code on the asset
4. AssetHub opens the asset detail page automatically

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the Scan page showing the camera viewfinder with a QR code in frame.

> **Tip:** Scanning works best in good lighting. Keep the camera 15–30 cm from the label.

### After scanning

You're taken directly to that asset's page. From there you can:
- Check it out
- Check it in
- Report a maintenance issue
- View its history

## 8. Maintenance Requests

If a piece of equipment is broken or needs attention, submit a maintenance request.

### Submit a request

1. Go to **Maintenance** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Ticket**
3. Fill in:
   - **Asset:** Which asset needs attention
   - **Title:** Short description (e.g., "Screen cracked")
   - **Description:** More detail about the problem
   - **Priority:** Low · Medium · High · Critical
4. Click **Submit**

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the New Maintenance Ticket form.

### Alternatively — from the asset page

On any asset's detail page, you can click **Report Issue** to open a pre-filled maintenance form for that asset.

### Track your tickets

The **Maintenance** page lists all tickets. You can see:
- Who reported the issue
- Current status (Open → In Progress → Resolved → Closed)
- Priority level
- Notes from the technician

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the Maintenance list page showing tickets with status badges.

## 9. Notifications

AssetHub sends you notifications for important events.

### View notifications

Click the **bell icon** (🔔) in the top-right corner of the screen. A dropdown shows your recent notifications.

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the notification dropdown showing a list of notifications, some unread (highlighted).

### Types of notifications you might receive

| Notification | When you get it |
|-------------|----------------|
| Asset checked out | Someone checked out an asset you manage |
| Maintenance updated | A ticket you reported changed status |
| Asset returned | An asset you lent out was returned |
| Invitation | You were invited to a new organization |
| Warranty alert | An asset you track is about to expire |

### Mark all as read

Click **Mark all read** in the notification panel to clear the badge count.

## 11. Your Settings

Click **Settings** in the sidebar to manage your personal preferences.

### What you can change

- **Display name** — how your name appears across AssetHub
- **Email address** — your login email
- **Password** — update your password
- **Theme** — Light or Dark mode (also available via the moon/sun icon in the top bar)

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the Settings page.

### Dark mode toggle

Click the **moon 🌙 / sun ☀️** icon in the top-right of any page to instantly switch between light and dark themes.

## 12. Switching Organizations

If you belong to more than one organization in AssetHub, you can switch between them without logging out.

1. Click your **organization name** at the top of the sidebar (it shows your org name and your role)
2. A dropdown appears listing your other organizations
3. Click the organization you want to switch to

> **🖼 IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Screenshot of the organization switcher dropdown in the sidebar showing two organizations.

Your permissions may differ between organizations — the tabs you see in the sidebar will update automatically to match your role in the selected organization.

## 13. Getting Help

### Something not working?

1. Check that your browser is up to date (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari)
2. Try refreshing the page
3. Log out and log back in
4. If the problem continues, contact your **AssetHub administrator**

### Can't see a feature?

Some features are only available to users with specific permissions. For example:
- Creating new assets requires *asset write* permission
- Managing members requires *member management* permission
- Adding categories or asset types requires *catalog* permission

Contact your administrator if you need access to a feature you don't see.

### Contact your admin

Your organization's AssetHub administrator is usually in your IT team or operations team. They can:
- Reset your password
- Change your role or permissions
- Add or remove assets
- Set up new categories and asset types
